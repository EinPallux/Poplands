### Added — Quality-of-life batch · Statistics page (post-1.0, user 2026-07-12)

### Added — Quality-of-life batch · Camera bookmarks (post-1.0, user 2026-07-12)

### Added — Cozy living-world batch · Day/night routines (post-1.0, user 2026-07-12)
- **The island now keeps a daily rhythm.** As night falls, Islanders stroll to the nearest home and **tuck in** — the avatar slips inside (hidden) while the window glow already lit at night sells "someone's home" — so the island goes quiet and peaceful (fireflies + lanterns still twinkle). By **morning they reappear** and resume wandering, and at **dawn they drift toward a gathering spot** (the nearest fountain/fire/statue, or the island's heart) for a "good morning" cluster before spreading out. A tucked-in neighbour stays **logically on its walkable doorstep cell** (the "always on walkable ground" invariant still holds under soak); a homing neighbour that can't reach a doorstep in time just settles where it is (robust, and hidden = invisible anyway). The `AgentRenderer` hides the avatar (and skips its mixer) when the new `AgentView.hidden` flag is set. A neighbour mid-visit on a bench gets up at bedtime, freeing the seat cleanly.
- **Verification.** `scripts/verify-routines.mts` (6 checks: by day nobody's tucked in, night → neighbours head home and tuck in via the real time-of-day seam, all can retire on walkable ground, morning → everyone's up, no errors) + 4 new `IslanderSystem` routine unit tests (tuck in at night then wake by morning with the invariant holding, daytime keeps everyone awake, force-retire is deterministic, a neighbour leaves the bench at bedtime with no claim leak). **224 tests + verify-routines + islander/furniture/requests regressions green;** check + build clean. ### Added — Cozy living-world batch · Islander requests (post-1.0, user 2026-07-12)
- **Your neighbours now leave little wishes.** Every so often a resident wishes for something nearby — a tree or flowers, a pretty decoration, a neighbour's home, or a little shop — shown as a soft **💭 thought bubble** floating over their head (plus a toast: *"💭 Rosie would love a tree nearby"*). Place a matching thing close to them and the wish is **granted**: they give a happy wave + a thank-you, hearts pop, a *"Wish granted!"* toast plays, and you get a small reward (Pops + XP). The bubble pops away with a flourish.
- **Strictly cozy & no-grind (GDD §7.5):** wishes are by item *category*, so they're always grantable with something cheap you already own — never a dead-end. They never expire, never nag (at most two float at once), and ignoring one costs nothing. Wishes are **ephemeral** — they re-roll each session, so there's nothing to lose or hoard.
- **`RequestSystem`** (three.js-free): owns wish bookkeeping + a spawn timer, reads live resident positions through an injected provider (the same pull seam WorldFx uses), and grants on `item:placed` when a matching def lands within reach of the wisher. Granting fires a `request:fulfilled` reward-event credited by Economy + Progression — the exact pattern as fishing/garden/museum. Content-as-data (`content/requests.ts`); the wish bubble is a new `WishLayer` modelled on the speech bubbles.
- **Verification.** `scripts/verify-requests.mts` (7 checks: homes welcome neighbours, a neighbour leaves a wish → a 💭 bubble floats over them, placing a tree nearby grants it → Pops reward + the wish clears, no errors) + 6 `RequestSystem` unit tests (roll a wish, grant on a nearby match, ignore wrong-category / too-far placements, auto-spawn after the interval, never wish for an absent resident). **220 tests + verify-requests + islander/furniture regressions green;** check + build clean. No save change (wishes don't persist).
